Dr. Mary Whetstone

One of the Most Remarkable Women in Minnesota's History

Home Physician Mycologist

Dr. Mary Whetstone did many things throughout her lifetime that would merit recognition and acclaim, yet there is little information about her in books or on the internet. Having done my own research, I wish to share her story and the impact she has made on Minnesota's medical system and the field of mycology.

Mary Becomes a Doctor

Mary Snoddy was born in Pennsylvania (Dauphin County) on August 24, 1849. She grew up attending public schools and then went to Female College in Granville, Ohio. In 1881, Mary got her degree from the University of Michigan. She attended vsrious clinics, in London, Paris, Vienna, Detroit, Boston, and New York.

She then served on the staff of the New England Hospital for Women and Children in Boston, Massachusetts. The Hospital's goal was to educate women in the study of medicine and train nurses to care for the sick. In this way, they could provide patients with competent female physicians. No doubt, this would leave a deep impression on Mary and set her on a course to continue to use her medical training for the good of the women and children in the community in which she lived. Soon, Minneapolis, Minnesota would be the beneficiary of her energy and skill.

In June on 1882, Mary and Dr. Allen S. Whetstone of Marshalltown, Iowa were married. He had attended the University of Michigan, as well, graduating a year ahead of Mary. They moved to Minneapolis where they lived out the rest of their lives. He preceded Mary in death by 20 years on April 19, 1909.

Mary died of pneumonia in her home, at the age of 80 on October 24, 1929. At the time of her death, Mary was the corresponding secretary of the Minnesota Mycological Society.
